Why is monitoring air quality important?

Monitoring air quality is vital for safeguarding public health and the environment. By tracking pollutant levels, we gain insights into potential health risks, allowing for timely interventions and informed decision-making. This proactive approach fosters healthier living conditions and aids in the development of effective pollution control measures.

Features of AQMS

The main features of the system are as follows

Real Time Monitoring

Real time reporting of Air Quality Index, pollutant measurements such as O3, PM2.5, PM10.

Forecasting

Predicting air quality indeces and pollutants.

Comparing different locations

Compare different cities and their AQI's.

Pollutants analysis

Pollutants degrade air quality by introducing harmful substances into the atmosphere, leading to adverse health effects, environmental damage, and overall degradation of living conditions.

AQI

AQI provides a comprehensive index reflecting overall air quality and associated health risks.

PM10

PM10 assesses larger inhalable particles.

PM2.5

PM2.5 measures fine inhalable particles.
